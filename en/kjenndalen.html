Kjenndalen

At the end of Lodalen we find Kjenndalen, Kjenndalstova and the Kjenndal Glacier. These are popular hiking destinations for many. Here too, there can be many photo stops before you reach the actual destination. The road runs along Lovatnet, which has the characteristic turquoise green color. Along the road are well-kept farms, nice campsites and other popular motifs, such as the old Breng Pasture.

Further into the valley you can stop and read about the tragic Lodalsulukkene in 1905 and 1936, where parts of Ramnefjell fell into the lake and created tidal waves that destroyed everything in their path and killed 135 people. There is a memorial plaque and a nicely landscaped memorial garden.

From here it is 10 kilometers to the parking lot at Kjenndalsbreen. Then there is only a short walk to the lookout point towards the glacier.

On the return trip you can stop at Kjenndalstova for a snack or coffee break.
