Håskreda

Håskreda is an avalanche that comes down the mountainside in the same place every year.

About the avalanche

Throughout the winter, snow builds up on the tops of the mountains. In the spring, when the snow begins to melt, it forms conrnices that hang over the mountainside. Eventually, the snow is too heavy to hold itself up. This causes large amounts of snow and ice to fall down the mountainside. You can hear a deep rumbling and the sound of trees cracking. For someone who has never witnessed something like this before, it can be a frightening sight, but the locals know that the avalanche has been happening in this place for hundreds of years, so no houses or buildings stand in the way. Something that can be scary is when tourists see the amount of snow and go over to play and take pictures, unaware of the danger of another avalanche.

In some years, when conditions are right, the avalanche can go all the way down to the river. In the case of large avalanches, the wind caused by the moving masses carries powdered snow with it and can cover the entire village in a blizzard. It does not last long, but can be quite strange to experience on an otherwise clear day.
